Three things that separate me from the average contract writer.

Hiring a technical writer usually means either (a) a strong writer who gets the code wrong, or (b) an engineer who can't write. Here's how I solve that problem.

Engagement Process

What the first two weeks look like.

No surprise invoices. No week-long silence. Here's the exact sequence of steps between your initial message and your first published piece.

DAY 0 · INITIAL CALL

30-minute scoping call (free).

Send the brief or the rough idea. We talk audience, outcomes, existing docs, and any constraints (style guide, CMS, internal reviewers). You leave with a written scope and a quote — not a vague estimate.

DAY 1–2 · SETUP

Environment access and style-guide onboarding.

I stand up a sandbox that mirrors your stack — cluster access, API keys, test accounts. I read your existing docs and any voice guide end-to-end before writing a single line.

DAY 3–4 · OUTLINE

Detailed outline with the decisions mapped out.

You approve the outline before I draft. It includes the argument arc, every code sample, and the specific claims each section needs to support. One revision round included.

DAY 5–9 · DRAFT

I build the samples, then write the piece.

Every snippet is validated in a runnable environment. Screenshots are freshly captured. The draft lands in your editor of choice (Google Docs, Notion, a PR) with inline notes on every non-obvious choice.

DAY 10+ · REVIEW & PUBLISH

Unlimited revisions until it passes your internal review.

If your first-round review comes back clean, we're done. If it doesn't, I revise until it does — at no additional cost. That's the guarantee above, written into every scope of work.
